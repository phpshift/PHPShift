# Code of Conduct

By participating in this framework, you agree to uphold this code of conduct and understand that PHPShift™ is governed by clear ownership, contribution rules and branding protections.

## Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Demonstrating respect and kindness toward others.
- Providing constructive feedback aligned with the framework's goals.
- Respecting the author's vision and direction for the framework.
- Acknowledging that some contributions may not be accepted, especially during early development stages.
- Reporting issues responsibly and helping improve the quality of the framework.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, abuse, or discrimination of any kind.
- Misrepresenting or falsely claiming ownership of PHPShift™ or its components.
- Attempting to rebrand or fork the framework under a new identity in violation of the framework's ethical protections.
- Repeatedly pushing unwanted changes or contributions that go against framework direction.
- Misusing the name "PHPShift" or misleading users about its affiliation with the PHP Group.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all spaces managed by the PHPShift™ framework including GitHub repositories, issue trackers, pull requests, discussions, Discord or chat spaces and any other community-managed spaces, both public and private.

## Reporting Issues

If you witness or experience any behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, or if you encounter any branding or license-related misuse of the framework, please report it by opening a confidential issue or contacting the framework author directly.

Feedback: [Open Form](https://phpshift.com/#feedback)

## Contribution Philosophy

PHPShift™ is currently in a **shaping phase** and is evolving under a defined vision. While feedback and ideas are encouraged, please note:

- Contributions may not be accepted if they conflict with the framework's goals or structure.
- Community members are invited to help shape the ecosystem around PHPShift™ rather than reshape it.
- Forks are allowed (per MIT license), but forks intended to **compete** with or rebrand the original framework are **against rules**.
