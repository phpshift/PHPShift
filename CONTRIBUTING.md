# Contributing

Thank you for your interest in contributing to **PHPShift™** - a modern PHP framework focused on simplicity, innovation and AI-assisted full-stack development.
This guide outlines the contribution process, expectations and guidelines to ensure that PHPShift™ remains consistent with its founding vision and standards.

## Project Vision

PHPShift™ is a clean, developer-friendly framework for PHP web development that aims to:

- Stay lightweight and easy to maintain.
- Offer a streamlined full-stack development experience.
- Integrate AI-driven developer assistance and code standardization.
- Maintain a consistent architectural philosophy shaped by its creator.

This is **not** a framework that evolves based on feature bloat or "merge-everything" contributions. All changes are carefully reviewed for long-term sustainability and alignment with the core vision.

## Maintainer & Authority

PHPShift™ framework is solely created, developed and maintained by **Irakli Gzirishvili**.

The maintainer reserves the right to **accept or reject** contributions without extensive discussion if they do not fit within the roadmap or design philosophy. This especially applies during the **early shaping phase** of the framework.

## Contribution Status: Limited

While contributions are welcome, **please note** that:

- **Most feature requests and pull requests may be deferred or rejected** during early development to avoid overcomplication.
- **Bug reports**, documentation fixes and meaningful suggestions are **highly appreciated**.
- Major refactoring or architecture changes are **unlikely to be merged** unless requested or invited.

## Types of Contributions That Are Welcomed

- Clear and reproducible **bug reports**.
- **Security issue disclosures** [Feedback Form](https://phpshift.com/#feedback).
- **Documentation improvements** (typos, clarity, grammar).
- Suggestions and feedback via [Feedback Form](https://phpshift.com/#feedback) or GitHub Issues.
- Carefully scoped improvements that align with the framework's goals.

## What Contributions May Be Rejected

- Features that introduce excessive complexity or break the minimal philosophy.
- Pull requests that duplicate functionality already considered by the maintainer.
- Code style changes that are subjective or outside the established conventions.
- Attempts to rebrand or fork the project under a competing name or identity.
- Contributions from accounts engaging in spam, self-promotion, or plagiarism.
